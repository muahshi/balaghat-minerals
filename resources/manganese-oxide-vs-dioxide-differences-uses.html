Manganese Oxide (MnO) vs Manganese Dioxide (MnO2): Differences, Uses & Specs

Buyer's Guide · Balaghat Minerals

"Manganese oxide" and "manganese dioxide" are often used loosely in trade conversations, but they refer to different oxidation states of manganese with distinct properties, grade ranges, and end-uses. Here's a clear breakdown for buyers comparing the two.

The Core Difference

PropertyManganese Oxide (MnO)Manganese Dioxide (MnO2)
Typical content range60–75% MnO70–85% MnO2
Common formPowder, granulesGranular
Primary industriesAnimal feed, fertilizers, ceramics, chemical manufacturingBatteries, water treatment, specialty chemicals
Oxidation stateMn²⁺ (lower oxidation state)Mn⁴⁺ (higher oxidation state)

Manganese Oxide (MnO) — Where It's Used

Animal Feed

MnO is a common trace mineral additive in livestock and poultry feed, supporting bone development and enzyme function. Feed-grade buyers typically require consistent MnO% and low heavy-metal contamination.

Fertilizers

Manganese is a micronutrient essential for plant photosynthesis. MnO is blended into micronutrient fertilizer formulations, particularly for crops grown in manganese-deficient soils.

Ceramics & Chemicals

MnO is used as a colorant and flux component in ceramic glazes, and as a feedstock for various manganese-based chemical compounds.

Manganese Dioxide (MnO2) — Where It's Used

Battery Manufacturing

MnO2 is a key cathode material in alkaline and zinc-carbon batteries. Battery-grade buyers are typically the most demanding on purity, particle size distribution, and consistency between lots — this is the highest-value application for MnO2.

Water Treatment

MnO2 media is used in filtration systems to remove iron, manganese, and hydrogen sulfide from water through catalytic oxidation.

Specialty Chemicals

MnO2 serves as an oxidizing agent and precursor in various chemical synthesis processes.

What to Specify When Ordering

  • Exact % of MnO or MnO2 required (e.g., 60-75% MnO vs 70-85% MnO2 — these are not interchangeable)
  • Form — powder vs granular, and particle size if relevant to your process
  • COA requirements — what parameters need to be reported (Mn, moisture, particle size, heavy metals if feed/fertilizer grade)
  • Packaging — bag size and type relevant to your handling/storage
