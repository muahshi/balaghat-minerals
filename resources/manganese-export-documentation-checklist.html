Manganese Ore Export Documentation Checklist: COA, BL, COO & More

Buyer's Guide · Balaghat Minerals

Whether you're a first-time buyer placing a 5 MT trial order or a long-term importer running 30+ MT monthly contracts, knowing exactly which documents should accompany a manganese shipment from India protects you from disputes, customs delays, and quality surprises. Here's the full checklist.

Core Documents for Every Shipment

DocumentPurpose
Certificate of Analysis (COA)Lab report confirming Mn%, Fe%, SiO2%, Phosphorus, moisture, and size distribution for the specific lot. This is the document buyers reference for pro-rata price adjustments if material comes in below the guaranteed grade.
Commercial InvoiceStates quantity, agreed unit price, total value, and payment terms (e.g., TT advance + balance against BL, or LC at sight).
Packing ListDetails of how the cargo is packed — bulk, bagged, container count — used for customs clearance and cargo verification.
Bill of Lading (BL)The shipping document issued once cargo is loaded. Original BL (or telex release) is typically required by the buyer's bank for payment release under LC terms.
Certificate of Origin (COO)Confirms the goods originate from India. GSP/SAFTA-eligible COO can reduce import duty for buyers in eligible destination countries.
Weighbridge CertificateIndependent weight verification at loading — relevant since many contracts state "weight at buyer's weighbridge will be final for payment," making accurate dispatch weight documentation important for both sides.

Additional Documents for Specific Situations

Insurance Certificate

Required for CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight) orders — confirms cargo insurance coverage during transit.

Third-Party Inspection Report

Many buyers, especially on larger contracts, request SGS or Bureau Veritas inspection prior to shipment as an independent check alongside the seller's COA. This is increasingly standard for buyers unfamiliar with a new supplier.

Understanding the "Analysis at Buyer's Plant is Final" Clause

Many manganese purchase orders include a clause stating that the analysis report at the buyer's plant — not the seller's COA — will be final for payment calculation, with a pro-rata deduction if the delivered Mn% is below the agreed specification. This is a normal industry practice for ore shipments and protects buyers from grade variability across a bulk lot. Sellers with consistent, well-tested ore have little exposure under this clause; it primarily protects against outlier lots.

Payment Terms Commonly Seen

  • 100% Advance: Common for trial orders or new buyer relationships, often via TT (wire transfer)
  • TT 30% advance + 70% against BL: Standard structure for established export relationships
  • LC at Sight: Used for larger contracts, providing payment security to the seller upon presentation of shipping documents
  • GST: Applicable at 18% on domestic transactions; export transactions typically follow zero-rated/LUT provisions under GST law — buyers should confirm current treatment with their supplier
